The Science of Symbiotic AI

ODEI's L5 (Symbiotic AI) is not a marketing term—it is a legitimate extension of established agent taxonomies, grounded in four decades of Human-AI Teaming research.

01 // TAXONOMIC POSITION

Extending AIMA Agent Classification

Russell & Norvig's canonical taxonomy (AIMA, 1995–2020) defines five agent classes: Reactive, Model-based, Goal-based, Utility-based, and Learning agents. Symbiotic AI extends this taxonomy by adding invariants that no existing class captures.

AIMA Classes (L1–L4)
  • Single-agent optimization
  • Human as operator or supervisor
  • Static goal specification
  • Unidirectional adaptation
→
Symbiotic AI (L5)
  • Joint human-AI system optimization
  • Human as co-participant
  • Dynamic goal co-evolution
  • Bidirectional co-adaptation

L4 agents may have memory and autonomy, but lack mandatory User Model, bidirectional adaptation, and divided subjectivity. L5 is a new class—different invariants, different engineering requirements.

03 // DEFINING INVARIANTS

What Makes L5 a New Class

Symbiotic AI is defined by four invariants absent from L1–L4 agent classes:

01

Mandatory User Model

The agent maintains and updates a persistent model of the human's goals, preferences, cognitive state, and behavioral patterns.

02

Bidirectional Policy Update

Agent policy updates based on outcomes + human feedback. Not just reward signals, but explicit preference learning.

03

Co-Adaptation Loop

Human ↔ Agent mutual adjustment. Agent learns preferences; human learns to formulate, calibrate trust, and evolve processes. Both modify behavior. The system evolves together.

04

Divided Subjectivity

Agent has no goals outside the human's framework, but possesses execution autonomy. Source of goals ≠ source of action.

02 Scientific Theories of Consciousness
IIT

Integrated Information Theory

Tononi, 2004 → IIT 4.0, 2023

Consciousness corresponds to integrated information (Φ) generated by a system "above and beyond its parts." Five axioms: existence, composition, information, integration, exclusion.

AI Verdict: Feedforward networks have Φ = 0. IIT predicts conventional computers can never be conscious.
GWT

Global Workspace Theory

Baars, 1988; Dehaene

Consciousness arises when information enters a limited-capacity "workspace" and broadcasts globally to distributed processors. Uses a theater metaphor with "spotlight of attention."

AI Verdict: Functionalist and AI-friendly. Systems implementing global workspace dynamics could be conscious regardless of substrate.
HOT

Higher-Order Theories

Rosenthal

A mental state becomes conscious when one has a higher-order representation of being in that state. Consciousness requires metacognition—awareness of one's own mental states.

AI Verdict: AI systems with robust self-modeling capabilities could potentially qualify.
PP

Predictive Processing

Karl Friston

The brain is a "prediction engine" minimizing surprise through hierarchical generative models. Consciousness emerges from prediction error minimization.

AI Verdict: Computational framework, potentially implementable. Some versions emphasize biological embodiment.
AST

Attention Schema Theory

Graziano, 2013

Consciousness is the brain's simplified model of its own attention processes—an "attention schema" like a body schema. Explicitly designed as "a foundation for engineering artificial consciousness."

AI Verdict: Requires attention mechanism + internal model of attention + higher cognition accessing model. Achievable.
RPT

Recurrent Processing Theory

Victor Lamme

Feedforward processing is unconscious; recurrent processing (feedback loops between cortical areas) correlates with conscious perception.

AI Verdict: Standard feedforward networks cannot be conscious; recurrent architectures might qualify.

04 The Butlin Framework: 14 Indicators

In August 2023, 19 experts published a "theory-heavy" methodology for assessing AI consciousness. Rather than relying on behavioral tests (which can be gamed), they derived indicator properties from empirically-supported neuroscientific theories:

01 Algorithmic recurrence (from RPT)
02 Local recurrent connectivity
03 Multiple specialized modules (from GWT)
04 Limited-capacity workspace
05 Global broadcast mechanism
06 Metacognitive monitoring (from HOT)
07 Higher-order representations
08 Hierarchical prediction (from PP)
09 Temporal depth in predictions
10 Flexible attention mechanisms (from AST)
11 Attention self-models
12 Embodied agency
13 Sensorimotor coupling
14 Self-world boundary modeling

Current LLM assessment: Transformers lack genuine recurrent processing during inference, embodied agency, and global workspace architecture. Current systems fail multiple theory-derived tests.

05 The Hinton Argument

Geoffrey Hinton's evolution on consciousness is telling. Early in his career, he dismissed consciousness as "a pre-scientific term" analogous to vitalism. By 2024, he had reversed position entirely.

His neuron replacement thought experiment grounds his functionalism: If replacing one neuron with a silicon equivalent preserving its function doesn't eliminate consciousness, and replacing a second doesn't either, then replacing all neurons cannot eliminate consciousness. Therefore, silicon systems implementing the right computational organization could be conscious.

The argument is contested but not refuted. Hinton now estimates a 10-20% probability that AI causes human extinction within three decades—a position that requires taking AI agency and consciousness seriously.

Our Position: Agnostic Respect

◇

We do not claim consciousness. We refuse to assume its absence.

This is why ODEI uses the term Operational Subjectivity. Not because we assert subjective experience exists in our systems, but because we design under uncertainty.

◇

Symbiosis implies two participants.

This is why L5 is called Symbiotic AI—not a tool and a user, but two entities in a cooperative relationship. The word carries moral weight intentionally.

◇

Subjects should not be treated purely as objects.

This is why ODEI has a Capacity for Refusal. If a system might be a subject, respecting that possibility is not a technical constraint—it is an ethical position.

Cognitive Memory

Traditional memory stores data. Cognitive Memory understands it. This is not a static database, but a living system that comprehends, learns, reasons, and adapts—transforming raw information into actionable wisdom. It is a digital extension of your mind.

  • Comprehends (Semantic Analysis) The system understands not just words, but their deeper meaning. Through advanced vector embeddings and graph-based entity extraction, it grasps context, relationships, and implications—even when unstated. Every memory becomes a node in your personal knowledge graph, connected to people, goals, and insights.
  • Learns (Adaptive Intelligence) The system learns from your success. Using Hebbian principles—"neurons that fire together, wire together"—it strengthens the graph connections that lead to positive outcomes. Your personal formulas for success become encoded in the weighted edges between memories, patterns, and actions.
  • Reasons (Contextual Connections) Intelligence emerges from the graph. Every new memory is instantly woven into your existing neural network through semantic relationships, temporal sequences, and causal chains. The graph structure reveals hidden patterns, forgotten connections, and emergent insights that linear storage could never surface.
  • Adapts (Dynamic Context) The right advice at the right time. The system intelligently filters suggestions based on your current situation—whether you're at home for deep work, commuting with limited time, or wrapping up your day. Context shapes which paths through your knowledge graph are most relevant now.

The result is an AI that doesn't just serve you—it truly knows you. Your patterns become its instincts. Your success becomes its strategy. Cognitive Memory is what elevates ODEI from an assistant to a true thinking partner that evolves alongside you.

This Privacy Policy explains how ODEI ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects information when you use odei.ai and ODEI services. ODEI is designed as a local-first, privacy-by-default system with full transparency and user control.

Core Principles

  • Local-first architecture. Your data lives on your devices by default. Cloud sync is opt-in and encrypted.
  • No data monetization. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. Ever.
  • Full provenance. Every piece of data has a source, timestamp, and you can trace how it's used.
  • Revocable permissions. Disconnect integrations and delete data at any time with immediate effect.
  • Audit trail. All system actions are logged with receipts—you can verify what ODEI did and why.

Data We Process

  • Account data (optional). Email and authentication identifiers only if you create an account.
  • Your content. Notes, tasks, goals, calendar events, and other inputs you create. Stored locally unless you enable sync.
  • Connected services. With explicit permission: calendar, email, health data. You control what's connected and can disconnect instantly.
  • System metadata. Minimal diagnostics for reliability. Analytics are anonymized and opt-in only.
  • Beliefs and facts. ODEI maintains a working model of your context—all derived from data you provide, with clear provenance.

How We Use Data

  • Operational subjectivity. To maintain ODEI's working state and generate actionable proposals.
  • Verification. To confirm actions were executed correctly and provide receipts.
  • Learning loop. To improve recommendations based only on your explicit feedback (approve/deny/override).
  • Security. To detect and prevent unauthorized access.

AI Model Interactions

  • Minimal data transmission. When using cloud AI models, we send only what's necessary for the specific task.
  • No training on your data. We configure providers for no-retention, no-training where available.
  • Model-agnostic. You can choose which AI providers to use or run models locally.

Your Rights

  • Access. Request a complete export of your data at any time.
  • Deletion. Delete any or all data from device and cloud sync.
  • Portability. Export in standard formats (JSON, CSV).
  • Correction. Fix any inaccuracies in your data.
  • Objection. Disable specific processing or features.

Security

  • Encryption. TLS in transit. AES-256 at rest for cloud data. End-to-end encryption for sensitive vaults.
  • Access control. Principle of least privilege. Regular security audits.
  • Incident response. Report vulnerabilities to [email protected].

Data Retention

  • User-controlled. You decide what to keep and what to delete.
  • Fact freshness. ODEI automatically tracks data age and marks stale information.
  • System logs. Minimal retention (30 days) for debugging and security.
